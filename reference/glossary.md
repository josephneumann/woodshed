# Guitar Terms Glossary

> Consolidated from the Paul Davids course plus standard pedagogy. Used by The
> Instructor for precise, consistent language. Add terms as the curriculum grows.

## Fretboard & pitch
- **Sharp (#)** — one fret up; raises a note a semitone (half step).
- **Flat (♭)** — one fret down; lowers a note a semitone.
- **Semitone / half step** — 1 fret. **Whole tone / whole step** — 2 frets.
- **Octave** — interval of 8 scale notes sharing the same name (low C → high C).
- **Interval** — the distance in pitch between two notes.
- **Function** — a note/chord's relationship to the tonal center (key), expressed as a number (degree), can be flattened/sharpened.

## Chords
- **Root note** — the first note a chord is named from (root of E major = E).
- **Triad** — a chord of 3 unique notes. Major = 1–3–5; minor = 1–♭3–5.
- **1st inversion** — lowest note is the 3rd. **2nd inversion** — lowest note is the 5th.
- **Barre chord** — movable shape fretted with a finger barring multiple strings.
- **7th chords:** maj7 = 1–3–5–7; m7 = 1–♭3–5–♭7; dom7 ("7") = 1–3–5–♭7. The 5th is often omittable.
- **Add chord** — triad + one note from the next octave (Cadd9 = 1–3–5–9).
- **Extended chord** — a 7th chord plus 9/11/13 extensions; leave notes out as needed.
- **CAGED** — using open C-A-G-E-D shapes as movable shapes to map chords up the neck.
- **Chord progression** — the sequence of chord degrees in a song.
- **Key** — the group of pitches (scale) a song uses.
- **Transpose** — move a song/progression up or down by a fixed interval.

## Scales & modes (formulas from the root)
- **Major (Ionian)** — W W H W W W H. **Natural minor (Aeolian)** — W H W W H W W.
- **Harmonic minor** — W H W W H (3 semitones) H (natural minor, raised 7).
- **Dorian** — W H W W W H W (natural minor, raised 6); 2nd degree of major.
- **Mixolydian** — W W H W W H W (major, ♭7); 5th degree of major; "dominant scale."
- **Major pentatonic** — 1–2–3–5–6. **Minor pentatonic** — 1–♭3–4–5–♭7.
- **Blues scale** — minor pentatonic + the ♭5 "blue note."
- **Relative keys** — share the same notes, different roots (Am ↔ C; Em ↔ G).
- **Blue note** — a tension note, often a lower-than-expected pitch (♭3 over a dom7).

## Technique & articulation
- **Alternate picking** — strict down-up-down-up.
- **Legato** — smooth, connected notes (often via hammer-ons/pull-offs).
- **Hammer-on** — sound a higher note by fretting without re-picking.
- **Pull-off** — sound a lower note by lifting a finger without re-picking.
- **Bend** — push a string to raise pitch (full = whole tone / 2 frets; ½ = semitone).
- **Vibrato** — repeated quick bending for character.
- **Tremolo picking** — fast alternating up/down strokes on one note.
- **Double stop** — two notes played together.
- **Grace note** — an ornamental note, not essential to melody/harmony.
- **Arpeggio** — the notes of a chord played one at a time.
- **Staccato** — short, detached notes.
- **Target note** — a chord tone of the current chord; resolves tension, feels like "home."

## Rhythm & time
- **BPM** — beats per minute (tempo).
- **16th note** — a quarter of a beat, counted "1 e & a 2 e & a."
- **Triplet** — three equal notes in the space of one (count "one-two-three").
- **Syncopated rhythm** — accents/notes on unexpected (off) beats.
- **6/8 time** — bar of six 8th notes, two groups of three, accents on 1 and 4.
- **Pick-up bar / anacrusis** — note(s) before the first full measure.
- **Passing note / tone** — a non-chord note used to transition between notes.
- **Key center** — the set of chords belonging to one key.
